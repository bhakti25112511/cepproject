Understanding AQI and Pollutants (Theory)

The *Air Quality Index (AQI)* is a color-coded measure that indicates how clean or polluted the air is. It ranges from Good (0-50) to Hazardous (301+). For asthma patients, any level above 'Moderate' (51-100) can be a trigger.

  • *PM2.5 & PM10 (Particulate Matter):* Tiny particles that can penetrate deep into the lungs. They are major irritants for asthma and are often produced by traffic, construction, and burning.
  • *NO‚ÇÇ (Nitrogen Dioxide):* A gas from vehicle exhaust and power plants. High levels can inflame the airways.
  • *O‚ÇÉ (Ozone):* A gas, particularly at ground level, that can cause coughing, throat irritation, and breathing difficulty, even in healthy individuals.

What is Asthma?

Asthma is a chronic lung disease that inflames and narrows the airways, making breathing difficult. Symptoms include *wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing*.

Air Quality Triggers

For most asthma patients, the key triggers in poor air quality are *high levels of PM2.5, Ozone (O‚ÇÉ), and NO‚ÇÇ*. Using your BreatheEasy dashboard helps you anticipate and avoid high-risk days.

The Peak Flow Meter

A simple device used to measure how fast you can push air out of your lungs. Tracking this daily helps you *monitor your asthma control* and predict an attack before severe symptoms begin. It's vital for your Asthma Action Plan.

Controller vs. Reliever

Asthma medications are generally split into two types: *Controller* (taken daily to prevent symptoms) and *Reliever* (taken for quick relief during an attack). *Always* follow your doctor's prescribed action plan.

Indoor Air Quality

Don't forget indoor triggers! Dust mites, pet dander, mold, and strong scents (cleaners, perfumes) can also cause flare-ups. Use *HEPA filters* and keep humidity low to manage these.

Exercise & Asthma

Physical activity is important, but if you have *Exercise-Induced Bronchoconstriction (EIB)*, always use your reliever inhaler 15-30 minutes before exercising, especially when the AQI is high.

Symptoms of Asthma

Common signs and symptoms to help you recognize asthma early and during a flare-up.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing, often feeling like you can't get enough air, especially during physical activity or at night.

Coughing

Frequent, sometimes chronic, coughing. It can be worse at night, disrupting sleep, or be triggered by cold air or laughter.

Wheezing

A high-pitched, whistling sound when exhaling, caused by narrowed and inflamed airways. This is a classic sign of an asthma attack.

Chest Tightness

A feeling of pressure or squeezing in the chest, which can be alarming and is a sign that the airways are constricting.
